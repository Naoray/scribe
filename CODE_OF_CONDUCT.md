# Code of Conduct

Scribe follows a Contributor Covenant-style standard for community behavior.

## Our Standard

Be respectful, practical, and constructive. Welcome contributors with different backgrounds, experience levels, identities, and viewpoints.

Examples of encouraged behavior:

- Use clear, kind language in issues, pull requests, and discussions.
- Give feedback on ideas and code, not on people.
- Assume good intent, ask for context, and accept responsibility for mistakes.
- Keep disagreement focused on project goals and technical trade-offs.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, insults, unwanted sexual attention, doxxing, sustained disruption, or any conduct that makes participation unsafe or hostile.

## Scope

This code applies in project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, community chats, and any other forum where people represent the Scribe project.

## Enforcement

Report concerns to opensource@usescribe.dev. Project maintainers will review reports privately, respond as promptly as practical, and may remove comments, close threads, block users, or take other action needed to protect the community.

Maintainers should enforce this code fairly and consistently, regardless of a contributor's role or history in the project.
